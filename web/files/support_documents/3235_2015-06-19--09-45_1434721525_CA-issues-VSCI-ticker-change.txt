Company's Online Profile
BUSINESS:  Cogentix Medical, Inc. is a global medical device company, headquartered in Minnetonka, Minnesota, with operations in New York, United Kingdom and The Netherlands. The Company designs, develops, manufactures and markets innovative proprietary technologies serving the urology market.

Notes:  March 31, 2015 -- Uroplasty, Inc. (NASDAQ: UPI) and Vision-Sciences, Inc. (NASDAQ: VSCI) today announced the completion of their merger. The merger was originally announced in December 2014, and completion of the all-stock combination creates a new medical device company named Cogentix Medical, Inc. Beginning on April 1, 2015, Vision Sciences will change its name to the new company, Cogentix Medical, Inc. and it will trade on the NASDAQ under the symbol CGNT. Cogentix Medical will have its U.S. headquarters in Minnetonka, MN, where Uroplasty's current headquarters is located. In the merger, each Uroplasty share of common stock will be converted into the right to receive 3.6331 shares of Vision common stock. The exchange ratio will be adjusted appropriately to fully reflect the effect of any reclassification, stock split, stock dividend or distribution, recapitalization or other similar transaction with respect to either the Uroplasty shares or Vision shares prior to the completion of the merger, including the reverse stock split. Upon completion of the merger, Uroplasty shareholders will own approximately 62.5% of the combined company and the Vision shareholders will own approximately 37.5%. Based on the number of Uroplasty and Vision shares outstanding as of February 20, 2015, we expect that the total number of Vision shares to be issued in connection with the merger will be approximately 80.4 million. In connection with the closing of the merger, Uroplasty shares will be delisted from NASDAQ and deregistered under the federal securities laws, and shares of common stock of the combined company will trade under the symbol CGNT.________________March 31, 2015 -- Following the shareholders meeting of Vision-Sciences, Inc. (NASDAQ: VSCI) on March 30, 2015, the Company's board of directors determined to effect a one-for-five reverse stock split of the Company's common stock effective at 4:05 pm EDT on March 31, 2015.
